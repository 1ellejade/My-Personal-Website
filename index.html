Data-Driven Analysis and Optimization for Urban Energy Systems' Equitable Resilience

Research paper to be presented at the 2023 57th Annual Conference on Information Sciences and Systems (CISS), Johns Hopkins Unversity

May-Dec 2022 Gabrielle Ebbrecht

Abstract - Electric vehicles (EVs) can be leveraged as power resources to support the grid operation in challenging scenarios, e.g., natural disasters or health crises such as the COVID-19 pandemic. This paper aims to enhance equity of power resilience in urban energy systems by means of strategic allocation of EV charging infrastructure. We first use data-driven approaches to infer the relationships between communities' power resilience equity and available EV charging infrastructure as well as other prominent social-demographic factors. This inference leads to the development of a machine learning model for power resilience inequity prediction. We further develop an optimization framework that jointly considers equitable resiliency and resource utilization to guide the optimized EV charging infrastructure allocation across the city. Case studies demonstrate the capability of the devised approach in enhancing power resilience equity in marginalized communities.

Applying Minimum Spanning Trees for Nigerian Public Transportation Design

A solo project paper for Algorithms

Nov-Dec 2022 Gabrielle Ebbrecht

Abstract - The focus of this paper is to investigate the real-world applications of minimum spanning trees (MSTs) and utilize their related greedy algorithms in creating a public transportation design plan. The underdeveloped public transportation infrastructure in Lagos, Nigeria is used as a case study for this work. To this end, geographical data is leveraged in creating a network of Nigerian cities to be utilized in constructing an MST, and the resulting model is evaluated on the bases of cost and efficiency. Additional real-world considerations such as revenue are weighed and discussed to determine the model's usefulness in the context of network design.
