Interests

What I like to do in my free time!

Since I was old enough to spell, I have loved to write. My favorite pastime is working on my novels and exploring philosophical themes within the science fiction genre. I also love to read both fiction and nonfiction, my favorite series at the moment being the Three Body Problem trilogy by Cixin Liu (masterpieces, if you're looking for a good read).

I am a self-taught pianist and guitarist, and had the privilege of performing at the Jazz at Lincoln Center Rose Hall in 2017 after winning the Talented Young Musician Olympia Competition Gold Prize Award for piano. I also write music using both instruments and voice, some of which I have performed at recitals, recorded, and produced.

Though I'm not yet fluent in any foreign languages, my passion for learning them has been a constant throughout my life. My grandmother spoke to me in Spanish in my childhood, and since then I've dabbled in learning German in high school and the basics of Mandarin and Korean during college. I hope to reach proficiency in Korean as I have been learning the langauge on and off over the last eight years.

At the start of the pandemic I decided to take up coding. My first language was HTML, though in time I began programming with Python and exploring other programming languages. This inspired me to concentrate in Technology for my bachelors, which was my introduction to the sphere of data and information science.

When I'm not at a computer, desk, or piano, I enjoy adventuring outdoors and hiking, snapping plenty of pics along the way. Above is my favorite shot of the Mt. Pinatubo crater lake I hiked 8 miles to see in December!
